Think of an agent as an assistant that learns one job and does it for you — drafting your posts, handling routine replies, sorting your data, sending the follow-ups. The difference from "AI that just chats": an agent actually does the work.

Straight answers

How does paying work?

You start for $150 and pay step by step — each step has a clear price before I begin it, based on an honest hours estimate at $75/hr. No big upfront bill, no surprise invoices, ever. You only ever fund the next step.

Do I own everything?

Completely — the code, the accounts, and the agent. I hand it over with a walkthrough so you can run it. No lock-in.

Is it safe to let an AI agent run?

It never acts unsupervised by default. Human approval, a kill switch, budget caps, and a full audit log are built in. The understudy supports the lead — it doesn't replace you.

What if my idea doesn't fit a package?

The quote tells you honestly. If it's bigger, we scope it before you commit — and I'll tell you if you shouldn't build it, or if I'm not the right fit.

What about ongoing help?

Every build ships with handoff docs and a walkthrough. Operator Agents come with an optional monthly retainer for support, safety monitoring, and tuning.
